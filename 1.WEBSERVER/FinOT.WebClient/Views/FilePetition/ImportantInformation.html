What you should know to file your petition with the Rent Adjustment Program

Before you petition a rent increase, you should know:

  • When and why your property owner can increase your rent.
  • What is a decreased housing services
  • Which properties are exempt from the Rent Adjustment Program
  • What’s involved in the petition process, and the timeline of events

What you might need to file a petition

To file a petition, you must be current on rent or legally withholding rent, and it must be less than 90 days since you received the rent increase or the loss of housing services. If you did not receive a RAP Notice with the rent increase you are contesting but have received it in the past, you have 120 days to file a petition (O.M.C. 822.090.A.3)

NOTE: You have until 7 days before the hearing date to submit additional documentation for your case.

Here’s a checklist of what you, as a tenant, will need to file this petition:

  • Contact information for the property owner or property manager
  • Reasons you are filing the petition, based on a rent increase or loss of housing services.
  • If applicable, your rental history, including date of rent increase(s)
  • If applicable, description of lost housing services, date of housing service loss, and estimated dollar value of housing service loss
  • Optional: Evidence supporting your petition, such as copies of receipts, written notices, photographs and any correspondence with the property manager or property owner.
